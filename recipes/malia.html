Malai Kofta

Ingredients

For koftas

  • 1 cup grated paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tbsp cornflour or all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

For Gravy:

  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ cup fresh cream
  • 2 tbsp cashew paste (optional, for richness)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

To prepare the Malaika Kofta, start by making the koftas. Mix boiled and mashed potatoes with grated paneer, chopped coriander, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, cumin seeds, garam masala, green chilies, and salt. Shape this mixture into smooth balls. Heat oil in a pan and fry the koftas until golden brown. For the gravy, heat oil in a separate pan, and sauté cumin seeds and chopped onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, followed by green chilies and ground spices like turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and coriander powder. Stir in pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates. Add cream and cashew paste (optional), and cook for a few minutes to thicken the gravy. Carefully add the fried koftas to the gravy and simmer for 5 minutes so they soak up the flavor. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
